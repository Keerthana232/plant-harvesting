Tulsi (Thulasi)

Boosts Immunity

Description

Tulsi, also known as Holy Basil, is one of the most sacred and powerful medicinal plants in India. It has been used for centuries in Ayurveda for its healing properties. Tulsi is rich in antioxidants and helps the body fight infections naturally. It is commonly used to treat cold, cough, fever, and respiratory problems. The leaves are often consumed raw or added to herbal teas to boost immunity. Tulsi also helps reduce stress, improve mental clarity, and support overall health. Due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, Tulsi plays an important role in maintaining a healthy lifestyle. Growing Tulsi at home is considered both medically and spiritually beneficial.

Uses

  • Boosts immunity
  • Treats cold and cough
  • Improves respiratory health
  • Reduces stress

How to Grow

  • Needs sunlight(4-6 hours daily)
  • Water regularly but avoid overwatering
  • Grows well in pots and garden soil
  • Easy to maintain at home

Aloe Vera (Sotru Kattralai)

Heals Skin

Description

Aloe Vera is a well-known medicinal plant recognized for its thick, gel-filled leaves. It has been widely used in skincare, hair care, and natural healing remedies for many years. The gel inside the leaves contains vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. It is commonly used to heal burns, cuts, and skin irritation. Aloe Vera also helps moisturize the skin and improve hair strength. In addition, it supports digestion when consumed in small amounts. This plant is easy to grow and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for home gardens. Its cooling and healing properties make it an essential herbal plant.

Uses

  • Heals burns and cuts
  • Improves skin health
  • Used in hair care
  • Helps digestion

How to Grow

  • Requires bright sunlight
  • Water only when soil is dry
  • Use well-drained soil

Neem (Vembu)

Purifies Blood

Description

Neem is a powerful medicinal plant known for its strong antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral properties. It has been widely used in traditional Indian medicine for centuries to treat various health conditions. Neem helps purify the blood, treat skin problems like acne, and improve dental health. Its leaves, bark, and oil are commonly used in herbal remedies. It is also known for boosting immunity and protecting the body from infections. Neem grows into a large tree and requires space and sunlight. It is considered a natural purifier and plays an important role in maintaining health and hygiene.

Uses

  • Purifies blood
  • Treats acne and skin problems
  • Improves dental health
  • Boosts immunity

How to Grow

  • Needs full sunlight
  • Water moderately
  • Grows into a tree
  • Requires space to grow

Mint (Pudina)

Aids Digestion

Description

Mint is a fast-growing herb known for its refreshing aroma and cooling effect. It is widely used in cooking, herbal drinks, and traditional medicine. The leaves are rich in nutrients and provide several health benefits. Mint helps improve digestion, reduce nausea, and freshen breath. It is commonly added to juices, chutneys, and teas for flavor and health benefits. It also has calming properties that help reduce stress. This plant grows quickly and spreads easily, making it ideal for home gardening. It requires minimal care and can be grown in pots or garden soil.

  • Freshens breath

  • Used in juices and food
